Jian's Games

A series of games in Unreal Enginer 4 made for CIS 568: Master's Game Design Practicum

New

Jian's Games is a series of games made in Unreal Engine 4 by Sacha Best, Daniel McCann, and Jian Ru at the University of Pennsylvania.

Jian’s Temple is a modern, four player version of the classic cat and mouse idiom. Three players explore a dimly lit temple in search of a golden treasure hidden somewhere within. These three explorers can carry torches found on walls to light their way through the world while a fourth player, the temple keeper, attempts to keep them at bay. The keeper is a much stronger and faster creature that can convert explorers into other keepers on contact. However, the keepers are vulnerable to fire, so an explorer can choose to risk throwing a torch at a keeper to send it sprinting back to its underwater home to extinguish itself. But, if the torch misses and hits the ground, its light will be extinguished and the explorer will have a very hard time finding the creature again. The game ends when one of two conditions are met: either the creature has successfully converted all explorers into other creatures, or the explorers find the treasure. Trailer

Fast paced action meets thrilling cooperative gameplay in a new Oculus and Leap Motion powered VR experience: Jian's Spaceship. Two pirates are on the run from the Empire - an evil and overwhelming galactic force that seeks to encase both pirates in Carbonite and place them on display in the Emperor’s ballroom. Their mission: to evade and destroy Empire forces while on the run in surprisingly fast, yet broken down ship. One pirate serves as the gunner - manning a blaster cannon facing backwards in the ship to shoot down enemy fighters, while the other is the pilot, facing forward and guiding the ship through treacherous asteroids and debris. Both pirates have to overcome many obstacles along their path - ship parts will be in various states of disarray - both players must communicate to repair them appropriately while maintaining their primary responsibilities. Failure to do so will result in a ship breakdown, and capture by the Empire. Trailer

Epidemic Control Simulation: Ebola in Sierra Leone

How can we mitigate future viral epidemics through graph analysis and algorithmic planning? A team of three Penn students developed an analysis of the data behind the spread of Ebola through Sierra Leone. We condensed a graph of 10M nodes + 90M edges into a comprehensible visualization and concluded that removal of key edges could have prevented the epidemic.
